Solar computer

a processor formed in solid glass, that runs by reflecting and diffracting sunlight. perhaps it is formed by some wonderful undiscovered holographic principle, allowing them to be made from filtered sand on dunes and beaches with limited tools. its display is the same reflected/refracted light that runs through its circuits, lensed and projected onto dark walls and floors. programs and data are etched into pieces of glass or paper that block the light entering the data window.

I wrote this in response to a question posted on the Merveilles Mastadon instance, without having any understanding of the principles required. Even giving it a little more thought I can see all sorts of complications: light coherence, loss of intensity, no idea of what scale this would/could be implemented at, how to move beyond combinational logic.

But let's collect some notes.
